Management Defines the Prompts

Qualitative screen criteria live in a shared GDrive document. Anyone on the team adds prompts of interest. The agent reads them on every run.

prompt_2: "Current and end market growth trajectory"
prompt_3: "Management tone on capital allocation"
prompt_4: "Guidance credibility vs prior quarters"
prompt_5: "Key risks and their change in emphasis"

# Anyone on the team can add new prompts here.
# The agent picks them up on the next scheduled run.

New transcript drops? The qualitative screen runs automatically. This is the curation layer. The LLM is commodity. These prompts, shaped by decades of GBL judgment, are the proprietary edge.

Adoption Strategy

Mandated AI Usage Does Not Encourage Adoption

The most effective enterprise AI rollouts follow a curated discovery pattern, not top-down mandates.

  • Curated performance discovery period: let people find tools that genuinely help their specific workflow
  • Share, don't mandate: have them share what they found useful with each other
  • Define approvals needed, then come back a few weeks later
  • Collect the tools people actually used and bring to management to show organic adoption
  • Tier by seniority: different agents are relevant at each rung in the hierarchy
